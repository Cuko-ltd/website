## Marketplace Platforms

### Toptal
- Positioning: Top 3% vetting, global talent, any seniority
- Pricing: $500 deposit + hourly; no UK rate transparency
- Weakness: Skews technical engineer, not exec leader; generalist pool; no compliance/regulatory specialism; platform markup 20–40%; buyer gets a stranger, not a proven operator with a public track record

### Bolster.com
- Positioning: Exec marketplace for fractional/interim/advisory + board roles; VC-network adjacent
- Pricing: opaque; platform fees apply
- Weakness: US-centric; no EU regulatory knowledge; supply-side network, not demand-side; no content authority

### Catalant
- Positioning: Strategy consultants and senior execs for project work; enterprise-oriented
- Pricing: opaque
- Weakness: Leans strategy consulting, not engineering execution; no hands-on code delivery; minimal EU presence

### GoFractional
- Positioning: Broad fractional exec marketplace
- Weakness: Quality variance; no regulatory specialism; US-centric

### A.Team
- Positioning: Invite-only network of senior tech operators; project-based
- Weakness: Team-assembly model, not single trusted advisor; no compliance depth

### Fractional.quest (UK aggregator)
- Positioning: UK job board + placement for fractional roles
- Weakness: Supply-side platform; buyer sees many candidates; no differentiation possible; commoditises the operator

## Boutique Firms (UK-focused)

### Freeman Clarke
- Positioning: "Largest and most experienced team" (100+ principals); strategic CTO/CIO/CISO for mid-market
- Target: Manufacturing, healthcare, professional services — NOT crypto/DeFi/AI-native
- Content: Newsletter, case studies, NPS 82 claim
- Weakness: Zero mention of DORA/MiCA/EU AI Act; no hands-on coding; "team" model dilutes personal accountability; no pricing transparency; no regulatory engineering depth

### Raul Tudor (fractionalcto.london)
- Positioning: Ex-Raindrop CTO, fintech + pension SaaS, London
- Specialties: ISO 27001, SOC 2, GDPR, Open Banking, KYC/AML
- Strength: Named personal brand, clear fintech niche, compliance framed as sales enabler
- Weakness: No crypto/DeFi/MiCA positioning; no DORA; no AI Act; no hands-on code claim; single sector (fintech SaaS/pension)

### Giles Lindsay (London, fractionalctos.org listing)
- Positioning: Business agility, digital transformation, exec tech leadership; Fintech, Enterprise SaaS
- Weakness: Generalist framing; no regulatory specialism; no hands-on code

### Jason Margolin (London, fractionalctos.org listing)

## Big-4 / Large Consultancies

### Deloitte / KPMG / PwC / EY
- Positioning: Regulatory compliance advisory, digital transformation, risk management for large financial institutions
- Strength: Regulator relationships; brand trust; full DORA/MiCA frameworks
- Weakness: Minimum engagement size (typically 6-figure projects); no hands-on engineering; no code; billing at £2,000–£5,000/day for partner time; slow; committee-driven; cannot ship production systems; no startup DNA

### Attack angle for Cuko: "The Big 4 write the framework document. Cuko writes the code that makes the framework real." Frame as the engineering execution layer that sits below the advisory layer — or the alternative for companies that cannot afford Big-4 minimums.

## White Space Summary
- No named independent UK operator combines: crypto-native + healthcare + AI agent infra + DORA/MiCA/EU AI Act article-level depth + writes production code + London base
- Freeman Clarke owns "large team, mid-market" but has zero regulatory engineering depth
- Raul Tudor owns "London fintech fractional CTO" but not crypto/MiCA/DORA/AI Act
- Platforms commoditise; Big-4 overprice and under-deliver on engineering
